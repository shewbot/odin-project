Tonkostsu Broth

What is tonkotsu broth?

Tonkotsu broth is a rich and creamy pork bone broth. The name "tonkotsu" literally translates to "pork bones" in Japanese, which aptly describes the main ingredient used to make this flavorful broth.

Ingredients:

  • 4lbs of Pork Bones
  • Cold filtered water
  • Ginger (optional)

Steps:

  1. Soak bones overnight in clean cold water.
  2. Drain the water and scrub the bones of any lose bloody bits.
  3. Refill a stockpot with water and simmer the bones for 18 hrs.
  4. In the last 4 hours of boil the broth rapidly so that the gelatin, fat, and water combine into the signature white broth.
  5. Pour broht through a chinois and reduce until it is the consistency you prefer.
  6. Store in the refrigerator for a few days or freeze for months.
