Chasu

What is chasu?

Chasu is braised pork that is typically seasoned with soy sauce, sugar, mirin, garlic, and other spices. The pork is slow-cooked until tender and flavorful, resulting in a succulent and savory meat that complements the noodles and broth in a bowl of ramen.

Ingredients:

  • Pork Belly
  • Soy Sauce
  • Mirin
  • Sugar or Honey
  • Ginger
  • Scallion

Steps:

  1. Set a sous-vide bath to 145 degrees farenheit.
  2. Roll pork belly and tie with butcher's twice.
  3. Place pork belly and all other ingredients in sous-vide bag.
  4. Sous-vide for 16 hours.
  5. Drain liquid and use for it for ramen eggs.
  6. Let cool for 2 hours.
  7. Slice and serve as is, broil it, or use a torch.
